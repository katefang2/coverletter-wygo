Introduction

Dear Wygo Team,

Hi there! I've downloaded the apps, shown up solo to midnight runs, wandered through cultural markets, and built more spreadsheets than I'd like to admit trying to manage my social life after moving to Toronto as an international student in 2019. Finding genuine connection in a new city is harder than I thought — and the burden too often falls on the people brave enough to host.


When I came across Wygo, I immediately recognized it as the tool that should have existed all along. That's exactly why I'm writing.

Experience

"Translating ambition into clarity, and complexity into intuition — that's where I do my best work."

The work Wygo needs done is exactly the kind I'm drawn to. Over the past few months, I've been working with a technical founder who had built a sophisticated AI-assisted documentation system but hadn't yet found who it was truly for. I led the process of identifying the core user, defining what the experience should feel like, and prioritizing the features that mattered most — helping turn something complex and technical into something people could easily understand and use.

3 / 5

What I Bring

Four years of graphic design at OCAD, a master's in digital media from TMU, a Google UX Design Certificate, and years of hands-on work across:

UX / UI Design Branding & Identity Illustration Motion & Video Web Design Graphic Design Design Systems

I can move between a high-fidelity UI and a fun event poster without losing momentum — and I genuinely enjoy both. When I saw Wygo's avatars during sign-up, I felt it immediately: this is a team that knows how to play yet still takes craft seriously. That balance is something I've been chasing in my work for a long time.

4 / 5

Closing

Joining this early means owning decisions that matter, and I don't take that lightly. I work closely with engineers and researchers, ask a lot of questions, and bring ideas back quickly — not because I'm restless, but because I believe great design happens in the conversation between disciplines.


I want to be in the room where Wygo figures out what it's becoming, and I want to help build the design culture that carries it forward.


I'd love to show you my portfolio and talk about what we could build together.
